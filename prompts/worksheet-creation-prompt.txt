Check @Topic Matrix.csv to determine the topic that corresponds to XXX grade, for week N. Then, create two worksheets for that topic, to be used in two different sessions that week. The two worksheets should be independent of each other. The first one should approach the topic from a technical perspective, and the second one from a social perspective. Include discussion questions, real-life scenarios, and critical thinking activities in both worksheets.

IMPORTANT: Worksheets are SELF-CONTAINED learning activities. They teach concepts and skills on their own, with instructional value regardless of any computer-based project. Do NOT frame worksheets as "preparation" for a TBox or other project. Do NOT use: "Get Ready for:", "Preparation worksheet", "Before you sit at the computer", "ready to start the project on the computer", or "What you will do in the project". Use instead: topic-focused titles (e.g. "[Topic Name]: Technical Worksheet"), "What you will learn", "Check what you learned", and "you have reviewed the main ideas from this worksheet". Write learning objectives, not project missions.

To create the worksheets, look at the sample worksheets in the sample folder (including 1-grade, 3-grade, 5-grade), the @README-Worksheet.md, and the @WORKSHEET-GUIDELINES.md documents. Save the worksheets in the worksheets folder, in the corresponding grade subfolder, and week subfolder.
