# Touristic Destinations – Preparation Worksheet

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Give this worksheet to students **before** they start the TBox “Touristic Destinations” project on the computer.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes**. The worksheet suggests roughly 3–6 minutes per part so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.

## Files

- **Touristic-Destinations-Preparation-Worksheet.html** — Open in a browser and **print** (File → Print or Ctrl+P). Give each student a printed copy.
- **Touristic-Destinations-Preparation-Worksheet.md** — Source text. You can paste it into Word or Google Docs to adjust wording or layout, then print.

## What the worksheet prepares (conceptually)

- The project goal: promoting a city as a tourist destination via email and graphic materials.
- Main parts of an email: To, From, Subject, Message (body), Attachment.
- What spam is and how to stay safe (e.g. no passwords by email, be careful with unknown senders).
- What makes good promotional materials: text, images, shapes, colors, clear title.
- A short plan for their first email and a “ready for the computer” checklist.

After completing the worksheet, students are ready to start the computer-based project with a clear idea of email structure, spam safety, and design basics.
