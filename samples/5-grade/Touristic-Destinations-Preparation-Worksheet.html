Get Ready for: Touristic Destinations

Grade 5 · Preparation worksheet · About 25 minutes
Work with pen and paper. Use color pencils if you like. You do not need a computer or the internet.

Name: Grade/Section: Date:
How to use this worksheet
  • Do the parts in order from top to bottom.
  • Each part has a time so you know how long to spend.
  • Read every instruction before you write. You can do this on your own.

Part 1 – Your mission (about 3 minutes)

What you will do in the project: You will choose a city in your country and use email to share graphic materials (posters, flyers, images) that promote that city as a place to visit. You will also learn what spam is and how to avoid it.

Do this:

  1. Choose one city in your country that you will promote. Write its name in the box.
  2. In one sentence, why would someone want to visit this city?

Part 2 – Parts of an email (about 6 minutes)

When you send an email, it has different parts. Later, you will use them on the computer. For now, write the correct part next to each description. Use the word bank.

Word bank: To · From · Subject · Message (body) · Attachment

What it is forPart of the email (your answer)

Tip: When you write a real email, always use a clear Subject so the receiver knows what it is about.

Part 3 – Spam: what it is and how to stay safe (about 5 minutes)

Spam is unwanted or junk email. It can come from people you don’t know, offer prizes or ask for passwords, and sometimes contain viruses or scams.

Do this:

  1. Write 3 ways to avoid spam or stay safe with email.


  2. Which of these is probably spam? Circle A or B.
    A) An email from your teacher with the subject “Project: Touristic Destinations”
    B) An email from “Super Prizes!!!” that says “Send your password to win”

Part 4 – Graphic materials: what goes on a poster or flyer? (about 5 minutes)

In the project you will use a graphic design tool to make materials that promote your city (e.g. a poster or a flyer). Good promotional materials use text, images (or drawings), and sometimes shapes and colors.

Do this:

  1. List 3 things that make a good promotional poster or flyer.


  2. Sketch a simple idea for your flyer in the box below. Use stick figures, shapes, and a few words if you want. It does not need to be perfect.

Part 5 – Plan your first email (about 4 minutes)

Imagine you are about to send your first email to share your city as a tourist destination. Fill in the following as if you were preparing that email.

To (who would you send it to?):

Subject (one short line about your email):

In one sentence, what will you write in the message?

What would you attach? (e.g. a poster, a photo of the city)

Part 6 – Before you sit at the computer (about 2 minutes)

Checklist. Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.

  • □ I chose my city.
  • □ I know the main parts of an email (To, From, Subject, Message, Attachment).
  • □ I know what spam is and at least one way to avoid it.
  • □ I have an idea for my graphic material (poster or flyer).

When all boxes are checked, you are ready to start the project on the computer.
