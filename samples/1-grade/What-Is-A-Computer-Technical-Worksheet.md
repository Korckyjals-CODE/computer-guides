**Grade 1 · Worksheet · About 25 minutes**

Work with pen and paper. Use color pencils if you like. You do not need a computer or the internet.

## How to use this worksheet

- Do the parts **in order** from top to bottom.

## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** A **computer** is a machine that helps people do many things. In this worksheet you will learn what a computer is, where we see computers, and what they can do.

**Do this:**
1. **Have you ever seen a computer?** Circle: Yes or No
2. **Draw a circle** around one place you have seen a computer (e.g. at home, at school, in a store).  
   _________________________

## Part 2 – What does a computer look like? (about 5 minutes)

A computer can look different. Some have a **screen** (like a TV). Some have a **keyboard** (with letters and numbers). Some are big and sit on a desk. Some are small like a phone or tablet.

**Do this:**
1. **Draw a computer** in the box below. It can be any kind—a laptop, a tablet, or a desktop computer. Use your color pencils!

## Part 3 – Where do we see computers? (about 5 minutes)

Computers are in many places. At **school**, at **home**, in **stores**, in **cars**, and in **hospitals**. Even a tablet or a phone is a kind of computer!

**Do this:**
1. **Name 2 places** where you have seen a computer.  
   _________________________  _________________________
2. **Is a phone a computer?** Circle: Yes or No

---

## Part 4 – What can a computer do? (about 5 minutes)

A computer can do many things. It can show **pictures** and **videos**. It can play **games**. It can help us **write** and **draw**. It can help us **learn** new things. Grown-ups use computers for work too.

**Do this:**
1. **Circle 2 things** a computer can do (you can circle 2 of these):  
   *Show videos*  *Eat lunch*  *Play games*  *Help us learn*  *Swim*
2. **Name one thing** you like to do on a computer (or that you would like to try).  
   _________________________

---

## Part 5 – Computers need people (about 4 minutes)

A computer is a **tool**. It helps people, but it cannot think or decide by itself. We tell the computer what to do. We use the keyboard, the mouse, or we touch the screen. People are in charge!

**Do this:**
1. **Who tells the computer what to do?** Circle: The computer or People
2. **Draw a happy face** in the box to show that you learned about computers today.

## Part 6 – Check what you learned (about 2 minutes)

**Checklist.** Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.

- [ ] I know that a computer is a machine that helps people.
- [ ] I can name at least one place where we see computers.
- [ ] I know at least one thing a computer can do.
- [ ] I know that people tell the computer what to do.

When all boxes are checked, you have reviewed the main ideas from this worksheet.
