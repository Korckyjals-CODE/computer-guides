# Impact of Technology – Worksheets (Grade 8, Week 38)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of impact of technology fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Technology impact from a technical angle: intended vs unintended effects, systems thinking.
- Efficiency and automation: how planned changes can create new problems.
- Data trails: types of data digital systems collect and why they matter.
- Resource and environmental impact: energy, e-waste, device lifespan.
- Trade-offs when choosing technology (cost, privacy, features, reliability).

### Social worksheet

- Social impact of technology: relationships, access, jobs, and well-being.
- Digital divide: who benefits and who is left out when services move online.
- Communication and well-being: pressure, boundaries, and group norms.
- Ethics and responsibility: consent, privacy, and respect when sharing online.
- Personal and community action: digital impact pledges and supportive policies.
