## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** **Security layers** are different levels of protection around your devices and data. Think of it like a castle with walls, a gate, and locked doors inside. If someone gets past one layer, others can still protect you. In this worksheet you will learn about the physical, account, network, software, and data layers—and why having multiple layers helps keep you safer.

## Part 2 – Physical and account layers (about 5 minutes)

The **physical layer** means who can touch your device. Keep it locked when you are not using it. Use it in places where strangers cannot grab it. The **account layer** means your passwords and logins. Use a strong password and never share it with anyone.

**Real-life scenario:** A friend leaves their laptop unlocked in the school cafeteria while they get lunch. When they come back, someone has changed their wallpaper and opened their social media. Nothing was stolen, but the friend felt unsafe.

**Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing:** Why might one layer fail? What would have happened if the friend had also used a strong password and locked the screen?

## Part 3 – Network and software layers (about 5 minutes)

The **network layer** is about how your device connects to the internet. Home Wi‑Fi is usually safer than public Wi‑Fi (e.g. at a café) because you control it. A **firewall** is a barrier that blocks unwanted traffic. The **software layer** means keeping your apps and system updated. Updates often fix security problems. **Antivirus** software helps detect and block malware.

**Scenario:** A student uses free public Wi‑Fi at a mall to check their bank balance. Later they notice strange charges. Someone may have intercepted their data on the public network.

## Part 4 – Data layer and backups (about 5 minutes)

The **data layer** protects your files and information. A **backup** is a copy of your important files stored in another place (e.g. cloud, external drive). If your device is lost, stolen, or hit by **ransomware** (malware that locks your files), a backup lets you recover without paying or losing everything.

**Scenario:** Ransomware locks all files on the school computers. Only one student had backed up their project to the cloud. Everyone else lost their work and had to start over.

**Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing:** Why are backups a separate layer? How do they help when other layers fail?

## Part 5 – Putting layers together (about 5 minutes)

**Defense-in-depth** means using many layers of security. No single layer is perfect. If one fails, others can still protect you. Compare: **Setup A** has one very strong password but no backup and no screen lock. **Setup B** has a good password, screen lock, regular backups, and updated software. Which is safer if someone steals the device?
