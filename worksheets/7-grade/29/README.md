# Security Layers – Worksheets (Grade 7)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of security layers fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Security layers from a technical angle: physical, account, network, software, and data layers.
- Physical and account layers: screen lock, strong passwords, keeping devices safe.
- Network and software layers: Wi‑Fi security, updates, antivirus; public vs home Wi‑Fi risks.
- Data layer and backups: why backups matter; recovering from loss or ransomware.
- Defense-in-depth: putting layers together; why multiple layers are safer than one.

### Social worksheet

- Security layers from a social angle: people, choices, and responsibility.
- Sharing and trust: why sharing passwords is risky; safer choices in common situations.
- Skipping steps: ignoring updates, reusing passwords; who is responsible when things go wrong.
- Peer pressure and convenience: saying no without conflict; trade-offs of security vs convenience.
- Helping others stay secure: teaching family, reporting suspicious messages, designing household rules.
