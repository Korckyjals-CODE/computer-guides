# Product Design – Worksheets (Grade 10, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of **product design** (especially for learning tools) fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Distinguish **user goals** from **features** and explain why design should start from goals.
- Describe **information hierarchy**, **plain-language errors**, and **consistent labels** in learning interfaces.
- Identify **constraints** designers respect: accessibility, language clarity, low bandwidth, and error recovery.
- Discuss **tradeoffs** between speed and clarity, and simplicity and power, using notification and due-date examples.
- Apply ideas through a simple **paper wireframe** (boxes and labels only).

### Social worksheet

- Analyze **equity** when products reward speed, visibility, or constant logins.
- Connect **usage data**, **nudges**, and **transparency** to trust and questions for school decision-makers.
- Explain the value of **early student and family voice** and **stakeholder** inclusion in rollouts.
- Recognize **pressure tactics** (guilt messaging, hard-to-leave flows) and draft ideas for **fair policies**.
- Practice **critical questions** about who benefits when learning technology shapes daily school life.
