# Education Tech – Worksheets (Grade 9, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of education technology fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Describe **education technology (EdTech)** as systems schools use to deliver courses, collect work, give feedback, and communicate.
- Compare a **learning platform** (hub with many connected parts) to a **single-purpose learning app**.
- Explain **accounts** and **roles** (e.g. student, teacher, administrator) and why **school sign-in** is used instead of many separate passwords when possible.
- Identify kinds of **data** created during normal use (submissions, messages, logs) and why **permissions**, **visibility rules**, and **backups** matter.
- Recognize basics of **accessibility** (captions, readable text, keyboard-friendly design) and **security hygiene** (strong passwords, phishing awareness) in school contexts.

### Social worksheet

- Discuss **equity and access** when learning depends on devices, connectivity, time, and language support.
- Analyze **privacy and boundaries** around classmates’ screens, screenshots, and what organizations may collect at a high level.
- Apply **participation norms** for chats, comments, and group work in digital learning spaces.
- Reflect on **academic integrity** when using digital help (including generative tools) and how to show your own thinking fairly.
- Name strategies for **well-being and attention** (breaks, notifications, social comparison) while using learning technology.
