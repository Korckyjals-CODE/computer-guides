## How to use this worksheet

- Do the parts **in order** from top to bottom.
- Each part has a **time** so you know how long to spend.

## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** **Technology** means tools that use **electricity** or **batteries**—like a **tablet**, **TV**, **game console**, or **computer** at school. In this worksheet you will name technology you use, tell **where** and **when** you use it, and match each tool to the **job** it helps you do.

## Part 2 – Where I use technology (about 5 minutes)

You can find technology at **home**, at **school**, in a **car**, or in a **store**. Some things use electricity. Some things do not (like a bicycle or a regular book).

**Real-life scenario:** Sam uses a **tablet** for homework and a **TV** for a show after dinner. Both have screens, but they do different jobs.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** How is the **job** different even if both have screens?

## Part 3 – What I do with it (about 5 minutes)

People use technology to **watch**, **listen**, **learn**, **play**, or **talk** (like a video call). Each tool is best at some jobs.

**Scenario:** You want to hear a song quietly. Headphones help you **listen**. A TV might be too loud for others.

**Critical thinking:** Why might a **calculator** be a better choice than a tablet for quick math facts?

## Part 4 – My day with technology (about 5 minutes)

Your day has **morning**, **after school**, and **evening**. You might use different technology at different times.

**Real-life scenario:** Alex’s family has a rule: **no tablets at dinner**. Alex can still use other things if the family allows them.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What technology might Alex still use at dinner? (Think: lights, microwave, clock—not only screens.)

**Do this:**

1. **List or draw 3 times** in your day when you use technology (morning, after school, or evening).

(Draw or write here)

2. **Circle** one: Thinking about *when* I use technology helps me *use it on purpose* / *not care when I use it*.

---

## Part 5 – Right tool for the job (about 5 minutes)

The **same job** can sometimes be done with different tools. Pick the tool that fits the job best.

**Real-life scenario:** You want to **call Grandma** who lives far away. A phone or tablet with a video call can help you **talk** and see her face. A game console is not the best tool for that job.

**Critical thinking:** What could go wrong if you pick the **wrong** tool for a job? (Example: trying to read a long story on a tool made only for short games.)
