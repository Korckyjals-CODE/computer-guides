## How to use this worksheet

- Do the parts **in order** from top to bottom.
- Each part has a **time** so you know how long to spend.
- Read every instruction before you write. You can do this on your own.

---

## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** Using **technology** is part of life, but we need **rules**, **kindness**, and **balance** (not only screens all day). In this worksheet you will learn to ask **permission**, take **fair turns**, take **breaks**, and stay **safe** with trusted adults and **privacy**.

**Do this:**

1. **Name one rule** your family or class has about devices (e.g. “Ask before you touch”).
   _________________________

2. **Circle:** Sharing devices fairly is *important* / *not important*.

---

## Part 2 – Permission and limits (about 5 minutes)

Before you use someone else’s **tablet**, **phone**, or **game**, **ask permission**. Follow **time limits** your family or teacher sets. Limits help everyone get a turn and stay healthy.

**Real-life scenario:** Your cousin leaves a **tablet** on the couch. You want to play a game on it. Your cousin is in another room.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What should you do **first** before you touch the tablet?

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** the safe choices: *Grab it and start playing*  *Ask your cousin or a grown-up if you may use it*  *Hide it so no one else plays*

2. **Write one sentence** you could say to ask permission (e.g. “May I use your tablet for ten minutes?”).
   _________________________

---

## Part 3 – Fair turns and kindness (about 5 minutes)

Sometimes there is **one computer** or **one tablet** for many students. **Fair turns** mean waiting, using a **timer**, or taking turns without grabbing. Use **kind words**. Do **not** laugh at mistakes on someone’s screen.

**Real-life scenario:** Two students both want the same school computer. There is only one at the table.

**Critical thinking:** How might the **waiting** student feel if the other student **grabs** the mouse? What is one **better** choice they could make?

_________________________

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** kind actions: *Grab the mouse*  *Say “Two more minutes, then it’s my turn?”*  *Yell “Hurry up!”*  *Use a timer everyone agrees on*

2. **Write or draw** one fair way to decide who goes first (e.g. shorter turn first, rock-paper-scissors, ask the teacher).
   _________________________

---

## Part 4 – Balance and breaks (about 5 minutes)

**Balance** means you do more than screens: play outside, read, draw, sleep, and eat meals. Some families say **no screens at dinner**. **Breaks** help tired **eyes** and a wiggly **body**: stretch, drink water, look far away.

**Real-life scenario:** After a long game, your eyes feel tired and you feel wiggly.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What is one **break** you could take that still follows your class or family rules?

**Do this:**

1. **List 2 activities** you enjoy that do **not** need a screen (e.g. soccer, drawing, building blocks).
   _________________________  _________________________

2. **Circle** healthy break actions: *Keep playing for one more hour without blinking*  *Stand up and stretch*  *Drink water*  *Look at something far away if your teacher allows*

---

## Part 5 – Trusted adults and privacy (about 5 minutes)

If something online feels **scary**, **weird**, or **too good to be true**, tell a **trusted adult** (parent, teacher, aide). **Privacy** means you do **not** share your **full name**, **home address**, or **photos** with **strangers** on games or websites.

**Real-life scenario:** A game says, “Type your **home address** to win a free prize!”

**Critical thinking:** Why might giving your address to a game be **unsafe**?

_________________________

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** the safer choice: *Type your address*  *Tell a trusted adult and do not type it*  *Ask a stranger online for help*

2. **Name one trusted adult** you could tell if a message or game feels wrong.
   _________________________

3. **Circle** information you should **not** share with strangers online: *Your favorite color*  *Your full name and address*  *Your favorite animal*

---

## Part 6 – Check what you learned (about 2 minutes)

**Checklist.** Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.

- [ ] I know to ask permission before using someone else’s device.
- [ ] I know how to take fair turns and use kind words when sharing technology.
- [ ] I know to take breaks and do activities without a screen sometimes.
- [ ] I know to tell a trusted adult when something online feels wrong.
- [ ] I know not to share private information (like my full name or address) with strangers online.

When all boxes are checked, you have reviewed the main ideas from this worksheet.
