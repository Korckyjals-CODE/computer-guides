## Part 2 – Safe vs unsafe websites (about 5 minutes)

Some websites are **safe**. They are from grown-ups, school, or games you know. Some websites are **not safe**. A safe website may have a **lock** (padlock) near the address. When you are not sure, ask a grown-up.

**Real-life scenario:** Maya wants to play a new game. A pop-up says "Click here for free games!" Her friend says to ask mom first. What should Maya do?

**Discuss with a partner or think:** Why is it good to ask before clicking?

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** the safe actions: *Ask a grown-up first*  *Click any pop-up*  *Use websites mom or dad said are okay*  *Click "You won!" buttons*

2. **Draw a lock** in the box below to remember to look for it on safe websites.

## Part 3 – Links and pop-ups (about 5 minutes)

**Links** take you to new places on the internet. **Pop-ups** are small windows that appear. Never click if you are not sure. A grown-up can help you decide.

**Scenario:** A big button appears on the screen: "You won! Click here!" What should you do?

**Critical thinking:** Why might someone make a button that says "You won!"? (Hint: Maybe they want you to click. Is that always good?)

## Part 4 – Passwords and devices (about 4 minutes)

A **password** is like a key. Keep it secret. Do not tell friends. Use your device in safe places where grown-ups can see (e.g. the living room, not alone in your room with the door closed).

## Part 6 – Before you sit at the computer (about 2 minutes)

**Checklist.** Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.
