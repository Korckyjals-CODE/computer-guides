# Safe Internet – Preparation Worksheets (Grade 2)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Give these worksheets to students **before** they start the corresponding TBox "Safe Internet" project sessions on the computer. There are **two independent worksheets**—one for each of two sessions that week:
  - **Session 1 (Technical):** Safe Internet – Technical
  - **Session 2 (Social):** Safe Internet – Social
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.

## What each worksheet prepares (conceptually)

### Technical

- The project goal: staying safe on the internet by knowing what is safe and what is not.
- Safe vs unsafe websites: lock icon, asking a grown-up; real-life scenario (pop-up offering free games).
- Links and pop-ups: when not to click; critical thinking about "You won!" buttons.
- Passwords and devices: keeping passwords secret; using devices in safe places.
- What to do when unsure: stop and tell a grown-up.
- A "ready for the computer" checklist.

### Social

- The project goal: staying safe on the internet by being kind and knowing who to trust.
- Kind words online: words can hurt; real-life scenario (someone says mean things in a game).
- Strangers and trust: not sharing name, school, or photos; critical thinking about strangers pretending to be kids.
- When something feels wrong: trusting feelings; telling a grown-up.
- Helping others: what to do when a friend has a problem online.
- A "ready for the computer" checklist.

After completing each worksheet, students are ready to start the corresponding computer-based project with a clear idea of safe internet concepts, scenarios, and critical thinking activities.
