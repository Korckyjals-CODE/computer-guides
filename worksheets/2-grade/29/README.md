# Safe Internet – Worksheets (Grade 2)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of safe internet fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- What a safe website looks like (lock icon, websites grown-ups approve).
- Safe vs unsafe actions: when to ask a grown-up, when not to click.
- Links and pop-ups: critical thinking about "You won!" and similar tricks.
- Passwords: keeping them secret; using devices in safe places.
- What to do when unsure: stop and tell a grown-up.

### Social worksheet

- Kind words online: how words can hurt; choosing kind responses.
- Strangers and trust: not sharing name, school, or photos; critical thinking about strangers pretending to be kids.
- When something feels wrong: trusting feelings; telling a grown-up.
- Helping others: what to do when a friend has a problem online.
