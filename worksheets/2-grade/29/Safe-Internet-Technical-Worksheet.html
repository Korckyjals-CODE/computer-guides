Safe Internet: Technical Worksheet

Grade 2 · Worksheet · About 25 minutes
Work with pen and paper. Use color pencils if you like. You do not need a computer or the internet.

Name: Grade/Section: Date:
How to use this worksheet
  • Do the parts in order from top to bottom.
  • Each part has a time so you know how long to spend.
  • Read every instruction before you write. You can do this on your own.

Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

What you will learn: In this worksheet you will learn how to stay safe on the internet. You will find out what is safe and what is not. You will learn what to look for and what to avoid when you use a computer or a tablet.

Do this:

  1. Name 2 places where you use the internet (e.g. at home, at school).
  2. Draw or write one thing that helps you know a website is okay to use.

Part 2 – Safe vs unsafe websites (about 5 minutes)

Some websites are safe. They are from grown-ups, school, or games you know. Some websites are not safe. A safe website may have a lock (padlock) near the address. When you are not sure, ask a grown-up.

Real-life scenario: Maya wants to play a new game. A pop-up says "Click here for free games!" Her friend says to ask mom first. What should Maya do?

Discuss with a partner or think: Why is it good to ask before clicking?

Do this:

  1. Circle the safe actions: Ask a grown-up first     Click any pop-up     Use websites mom or dad said are okay     Click "You won!" buttons
  2. Draw a lock in the box below to remember to look for it on safe websites.

Part 3 – Links and pop-ups (about 5 minutes)

Links take you to new places on the internet. Pop-ups are small windows that appear. Never click if you are not sure. A grown-up can help you decide.

Scenario: A big button appears on the screen: "You won! Click here!" What should you do?

Critical thinking: Why might someone make a button that says "You won!"? (Hint: Maybe they want you to click. Is that always good?)

Part 4 – Passwords and devices (about 4 minutes)

A password is like a key. Keep it secret. Do not tell friends. Use your device in safe places where grown-ups can see (e.g. the living room, not alone in your room with the door closed).

Do this: Match the action to the situation. Draw a line or write the letter.

SituationWhat to do
Someone asks for your password
You see something strange on the screen
You have a password for a game

Word bank: Tell a grown-up   Keep it secret   Don't click

Part 5 – What to do when unsure (about 5 minutes)

When something feels wrong or you do not know what to do, stop and tell a grown-up. You will not get in trouble. Grown-ups are there to help.

Do this:

  1. Fill in: "If I see something strange on the internet, I will ______________."
  2. Draw or write the name of one grown-up you can tell (e.g. mom, dad, teacher).

Part 6 – Check what you learned (about 2 minutes)

Checklist. Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.

  • □ I know what a safe website looks like (e.g. lock, websites grown-ups said are okay).
  • □ I know not to click pop-ups I do not trust.
  • □ I know to keep my password secret.
  • □ I know to tell a grown-up when I am unsure or see something strange.

When all boxes are checked, you have reviewed the main ideas from this worksheet.
