## How to use this worksheet

- Do the parts **in order** from top to bottom.
- Each part has a **time** so you know how long to spend.
- Read every instruction before you write. You can do this on your own.

## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** In this worksheet you will learn how to stay safe on the internet by being kind and knowing who to trust. You will find out how to treat people online, when to be careful, and who to tell if something feels wrong.

**Do this:**

1. **Name one person** online that you know in real life (e.g. a friend, a cousin).

## Part 2 – Kind words online (about 5 minutes)

Words online can hurt, just like words in real life. We use **kind words** online. If someone says mean things to you, tell a grown-up. Do not say mean things back.

**Real-life scenario:** Someone in a game says "You are bad at this!" How does that feel? What can you do?

**Discuss with a partner or think:** Is it okay to say mean things online? Why or why not?

**Do this:**

1. **Draw a face** showing how you feel when someone says something mean online.
   (Draw here)

2. **Write or draw** one kind thing you could say instead of something mean.
   _________________________

## Part 3 – Strangers and trust (about 5 minutes)

People online might not be who they say they are. Do not share your **name**, **school**, or **photos** with people you do not know in real life. When in doubt, ask a grown-up.

**Scenario:** Someone says "I am a kid too! What is your name and school?" What should you do?

**Critical thinking:** Why might someone pretend to be a kid? (Hint: Maybe they are not a kid. Why would that be dangerous?)

_________________________

**Do this:** Circle what you would do: *Tell them my name*  *Don't answer and tell a grown-up*  *Ask them their name first*

---

## Part 4 – When something feels wrong (about 5 minutes)

Trust your feelings. If something feels weird, scary, or sad online, **stop** and **tell a grown-up**. You will not get in trouble. Grown-ups want to help you.

**Do this:** Fill in who you would tell if something felt wrong. Then draw their face or write their name.

**If something online feels weird, scary, or sad, I will tell:** _________________________

(Draw here)

**Discuss with a partner:** Why is it important to tell a grown-up even if you are not sure?

---

## Part 5 – Helping others (about 5 minutes)

If a friend tells you something bad happened online, you can help. You can tell a grown-up together. You can be a good friend by being kind and listening.

**Scenario:** Your friend says "Someone was mean to me online." What would you do?

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** what you would do: *Laugh*  *Tell a grown-up with your friend*  *Ignore it*  *Say "That's not nice, let's tell someone"*

2. **Draw or write** one way to be a good friend online.

## Part 6 – Check what you learned (about 2 minutes)

**Checklist.** Put a ✓ in the box when it is true for you.

- [ ] I know to use kind words online.
- [ ] I know not to share my name, school, or photos with strangers.
- [ ] I know to tell a grown-up when something feels wrong.
- [ ] I know I can help a friend by telling a grown-up together.

When all boxes are checked, you have reviewed the main ideas from this worksheet.
