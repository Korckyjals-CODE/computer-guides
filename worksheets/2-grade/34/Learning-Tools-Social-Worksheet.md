## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** When we use **learning tools** together, we need **fair turns**, **kind words**, and **respect** for other people’s work and devices. In this worksheet you will learn how to share tools, how to help classmates feel safe, when to take a break, and how to protect **privacy** (keeping your own work and passwords private).

**Do this:**

1. **Name one rule** your class has about tablets or computers (e.g. “Ask before you touch”).

2. **Circle:** Sharing learning tools fairly is *important* / *not important*.

## Part 2 – Fair turns and sharing (about 5 minutes)

Sometimes there are **not enough** devices for everyone at once. **Fair turns** mean we agree on an order or a timer. We wait without grabbing. We cheer for others when it is their turn.

**Real-life scenario:** Two students both want the same tablet for a spelling game. There is only one tablet at the table.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What is one **fair** way to decide who goes first? What could you **say** with kind words?

**Do this:**

1. **Write or draw** one fair idea (e.g. rock-paper-scissors, shorter turn first, ask the teacher).
   _________________________

2. **Circle** kind actions: *Grab the tablet*  *Say “Five more minutes, then it’s my turn?”*  *Yell “Hurry up!”*  *Use a timer everyone agrees on*

## Part 3 – Kindness during digital learning (about 5 minutes)

On screens, people can still **feel** happy or sad. **Peek** means looking at someone else’s answers without permission. That is not kind and it is not fair. Use **encouraging** words, like “You can do it!” or “Want a hint?” if your teacher says hints are okay.

**Scenario:** Jay notices Kim’s answer on her screen before Kim covers it. Jay wants to finish first.

**Critical thinking:** How might Kim feel if Jay copies her work? What is one **better** choice Jay could make?

1. **Circle** the best response: *Copy quickly*  *Look away and do your own work*  *Tell everyone Kim’s answer*

2. **Write one encouraging sentence** you could say to a classmate who is stuck (no answers—just kindness).

## Part 4 – Asking for help and healthy breaks (about 5 minutes)

If the tool is confusing, **ask a trusted adult** (teacher, aide, family) or follow your class signal for help. Staring at a screen for a long time can make **eyes** tired. **Breaks** help your brain and body: stretch, drink water, look far away.

**Real-life scenario:** After 15 minutes on a tablet, your eyes feel dry and you feel wiggly.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What is one **break** you could take that still follows class rules?

**Do this:**

1. **List 2 break ideas** that are safe at school (e.g. stand and stretch, look out the window if allowed).
   _________________________  _________________________

2. **Fill in:** If I am stuck and not sure what to tap next, I will ask _______________________ for help.

## Part 5 – Privacy and respect (about 5 minutes)

**Privacy** means your work and your **password** belong to you. Do **not** tell friends your password. Do **not** log in as someone else. Do **not** touch another person’s device **without asking**. Respect means **asking first**.

**Scenario:** A friend says, “Tell me your password so we can share one account—it’s faster.”

**Do this:**

1. **Circle** the safer choice: *Share the password*  *Say no and tell a trusted adult if they keep asking*  *Log in for them on your account*

2. **Write one sentence** explaining why sharing passwords is a bad idea (e.g. they could change your work or pretend to be you).
