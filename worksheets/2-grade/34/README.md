# Learning Tools – Worksheets (Grade 2, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of learning tools fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–5 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Name **learning tools** that are paper-based and **digital** (screen and sound).
- Tell how a learner **sends** something to a tool (tap, click, type) and how the tool **responds** with pictures, sound, or messages.
- Describe **steps** in a lesson and what **feedback** means for learning.
- Compare a **book** and a **screen tool** for the same learning job and reflect on why teachers choose different tools.

### Social worksheet

- Use **fair turns** and **kind language** when sharing devices or learning stations.
- Recognize **peeking** and copying as unfair; use **encouraging** words instead.
- **Ask trusted adults** for help when confused; plan **healthy breaks** for eyes and body during screen use.
- Protect **privacy**: keep **passwords** secret, do not log in as others, and **ask before** touching someone else’s device.
