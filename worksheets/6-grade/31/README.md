# Design Thinking – Worksheets (Grade 6)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of design thinking fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- The five phases of design thinking: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test.
- Why we empathize and define the problem before solving.
- Ideate (brainstorm many ideas) and prototype (make a simple version).
- How testing helps us improve our design.
- Applying the phases to a problem of your own.

### Social worksheet

- The user: who we design for and why understanding them matters.
- Different people have different needs; one design cannot always work for everyone.
- Working in a design team: sharing ideas, listening, building on others’ ideas.
- How design can help the school, neighborhood, or community.
- Identifying a real problem and a design idea that could help.
