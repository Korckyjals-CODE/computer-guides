# EdTech – Worksheets (Grade 6, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of EdTech (education technology) fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. The two sheets are **independent**—either can be used first in the same week. No computer project is required.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Explain **EdTech** and the role of a **learning platform** / **LMS** as an organized class hub.
- Identify common platform parts: **assignments**, **lesson materials**, **feedback**, and **progress**.
- Distinguish **input** from **output** in learning apps.
- Explain why **saving** a draft is not always the same as **submitting** work to the teacher.
- Compare **instant feedback** with **teacher feedback** and describe how **clear titles** help students navigate class posts.

### Social worksheet

- Describe **fair access** when devices or connectivity are shared at school or home.
- Practice **respect and privacy** around classmates’ screens and in **comments** or **chat**.
- Recognize **academic honesty** and appropriate responses when pressured to share finished work.
- Plan **fair roles** for **group EdTech** work and spot signs of unfair workload.
- Name **healthy break** habits and when to tell a **trusted adult** about unsafe or uncomfortable online school situations.
