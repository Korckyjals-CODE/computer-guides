# EdTech Business – Worksheets (Grade 11, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of EdTech business fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Explain how **buyers** and **end users** of EdTech can differ and how **B2B** vs **B2C** sales motions show up in real examples.
- Describe **revenue models** (e.g. subscription, site license, freemium) and the **whole product** (implementation, training, support).
- Identify why **integrations** and **implementation** plans matter during rollouts and who often acts first when problems appear.
- Name **product metrics**, **differentiation**, and **compliance** topics (student data, agreements) that serious purchasers investigate.

### Social worksheet

- Discuss **equity of access**, opportunity gaps, and **public** responses beyond individual purchases.
- Analyze **commercial pressure** from ads, upsells, and gamification in classroom contexts.
- Weigh **surveillance-style monitoring** against **trust** and **student agency**, including aggregate vs individual reporting.
- Connect EdTech to **global labor**, **infrastructure**, and **community voice** in procurement and oversight.
- Identify civic habits for participating in **fair** technology decisions at school.
