# Solve Creatively – Worksheets (Grade 3)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of solve creatively fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Creative problem-solving: what it means and how it works.
- Steps to solve creatively: understand the problem, brainstorm, try one idea, try again if needed.
- Brainstorming: thinking of many ideas; no wrong ideas at first.
- Trial and error: trying, learning from mistakes, and trying again.
- Applying the steps to a problem of your own.

### Social worksheet

- Creativity with others: sharing ideas and building on each other's thoughts.
- How creative solutions help people at home, school, and with friends.
- Respecting different ideas: listening, combining ideas, being kind when others think differently.
- Using creativity when people disagree: taking turns, mixing ideas, finding ways to include everyone.
