# Technology in My Day – Worksheets (Grade 3, Week 38)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of technology in daily life fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Explain **technology** as tools people design to help do jobs (not only screens).
- Notice technology across the day (morning, school, evening) and name examples.
- Match technology examples to jobs: **communicate**, **learn**, **move or find places**, **stay safe or comfortable**.
- Distinguish **input** (what the user sends to a tool) from **output** (what the tool shows or does).
- Describe that technology needs **power**, **care**, and safe habits; sequence steps for responsible tablet use.

### Social worksheet

- Explain why **fair turns** and agreed time matter when devices are shared at home or school.
- Practice **being present**—pausing screens and giving attention when people speak.
- Use **permission** before borrowing devices or accounts; name rules and non-screen activities for **balance**.
- Apply **kind habits**: sensible volume, ask before filming, tell trusted adults when something feels wrong.
- Rewrite unhelpful messages in kind, clear language.
