Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

What you will learn: Technology means tools people design to help do jobs—not only screens. Technology can be in your morning, at school, and in the evening. In this worksheet you will notice technology across your day, name what technology helps us do, learn simple input and output, and understand that technology needs power and care to work well.

Do this:

  1. Did you use any technology before breakfast today? Circle: Yes or No
  2. Name one technology tool you used or saw this morning (e.g. alarm clock, microwave, bus card, phone).

Part 3 – What technology helps us do (about 5 minutes)

Technology is built to do jobs for people. Four common jobs are:

  • Communicate — share messages, calls, or videos with others.
  • Learn — find information, practice skills, or follow lessons.
  • Move or find places — maps, GPS, tickets, or traffic lights that help travel.
  • Stay safe or comfortable — locks, sensors, thermostats, lights, or medical tools.

Part 4 – Input and output in everyday tools (about 5 minutes)

Input is what you send to a tool: you press a button, turn a dial, type, tap, or speak. Output is what the tool sends back to you: light, sound, heat, words on a screen, or movement.

Real-life scenario: You press "2 min" on a microwave (input). The microwave beeps and heats your food (output).

Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing: Why does it help to know what you control (input) and what the tool does (output)? What might go wrong if you press the wrong button?

Part 5 – When technology needs help (about 5 minutes)

Technology needs power (electricity or batteries), charging or fresh batteries, care (clean screens, gentle hands, no food or drink on devices), and sometimes updates from a grown-up. People also follow rules so tools last and stay safe.

Scenario: The power goes out at home. Lights, Wi‑Fi routers, and many chargers stop working. A battery-powered flashlight still works.

Critical thinking: Name one thing you can do to take care of a device or tool at home (e.g. charge when low, wash hands before touching a tablet).
