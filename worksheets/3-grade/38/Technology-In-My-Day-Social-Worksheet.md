# Technology in My Day: Social Worksheet

**Grade 3 · Worksheet · About 25 minutes**  
*Work with pen and paper. Use color pencils if you like. You do not need a computer or the internet.*

---

## How to use this worksheet

- Do the parts **in order** from top to bottom.
- Each part has a **time** so you know how long to spend.
- Read every instruction before you write. You can do this on your own.

---

## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** Technology is part of daily life **with other people**—family, friends, and classmates. How we act matters: we share fairly, stay present, ask permission, and balance screen time with other activities. In this worksheet you will learn about **fair turns**, **being present**, **asking permission**, **healthy balance**, and **kind habits** when technology is around all day.

## Part 2 – Fair turns at home and school (about 5 minutes)

When tablets, TVs, or game consoles are shared, **taking turns** keeps things fair. A timer, a list on the fridge, or a teacher's plan can show whose turn it is. Keeping extra time or skipping someone's turn hurts trust.

**Real-life scenario:** Two siblings share one tablet. Each gets 15 minutes. When the timer beeps, one child keeps playing because "I am almost at the next level."

**Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing:** How does keeping extra time affect the other person? What is a fair way to handle "just one more minute" without breaking the agreement?

## Part 3 – Being present with people (about 5 minutes)

**Being present** means giving attention to people near you—not only to a screen. When a parent, teacher, or friend speaks to you, it is respectful to **pause** your game or video and look up. Ignoring someone while you keep playing can feel hurtful.

**Scenario:** At dinner, a parent asks how your day was. You keep looking at a game on a tablet and do not answer until they ask again.

**Critical thinking:** What could you do instead that shows respect? Why can pausing for a minute matter more than winning one more level?

## Part 4 – Permission and balance (about 5 minutes)

Use **permission** before you borrow someone else's phone, tablet, or account. Families and schools often have **rules** about screen time, homework first, and bedtime without screens. **Balance** means making time for play outside, reading, sports, and rest—not only screens.

**Real-life scenario:** A friend wants to play on your tablet right now. They say you do not need to ask a grown-up because "we do it all the time."

**Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing:** Why is asking a trusted adult important even if a friend says it is fine? What could go wrong if you share without permission?

## Part 5 – Kind habits all day long (about 5 minutes)

Kind habits with technology include: **sensible volume** in headphones, **asking before filming** or photographing others, and **telling a trusted adult** if something online feels scary or mean. The same polite words you use face to face belong in messages and games too.

**Scenario:** During recess, a classmate films you with their phone without asking. You feel uncomfortable and want them to stop.

**Critical thinking:** What are **two safe steps** you can take (e.g. say stop, walk away, tell an adult)?
