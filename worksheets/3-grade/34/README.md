# Digital Learning – Worksheets (Grade 3, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of digital learning fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; optional hands-on activities may complement them if you choose.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Explain **digital learning** as learning with digital tools (screens, sound, stored lessons and practice).
- Identify common parts of a digital lesson: **instructions**, **practice**, **feedback**, and **progress**.
- Distinguish **input** (what the learner sends to the tool) from **output** (what the tool shows or plays back).
- Describe why **clear names** and sensible **order** help students find and continue their work.
- Sequence the steps of a simple digital lesson flow and represent learning with a quick sketch.

### Social worksheet

- Explain why **fair turns** and agreed time matter when devices or stations are shared.
- Recognize **privacy** on classmates’ screens and practice **asking before looking** or touching someone else’s device.
- Write or choose **kind, clear** messages appropriate to digital learning spaces (comments, chat, questions).
- Know to **ask trusted adults** for help and **not to share passwords** or log in as someone else; respond safely to pressure.
- Name **healthy break** habits that support focus and well-being during digital learning.
