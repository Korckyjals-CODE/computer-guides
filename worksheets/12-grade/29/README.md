# Cyber Strategy – Preparation Worksheets (Grade 12)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Give these worksheets to students **before** they start the corresponding TBox “Cyber Strategy” project sessions on the computer. There are **two independent worksheets**—one for each of two sessions that week:
  - **Session 1 (Technical):** Cyber Strategy – Technical Foundations
  - **Session 2 (Social):** Cyber Strategy – Social Dimensions
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.

## What each worksheet prepares (conceptually)

### Technical Foundations

- The project goal: cyber strategy from a technical angle (threat modeling, risk assessment, security architecture).
- Threat modeling and risk: threat actors, attack surfaces, risk ranking; real-life scenario (ransomware).
- Defense-in-depth: layered security, why multiple layers matter.
- Incident response: Detect, Respond, Recover; ordering steps in a breach scenario.
- Security trade-offs: cost vs. security vs. usability.
- A “ready for the computer” checklist.

### Social Dimensions

- The project goal: cyber strategy from a social angle (people, policy, culture, ethics).
- Human factors in security: phishing, awareness; scenario (employee falls for phishing).
- Policy and responsibility: individual, organization, government; shared responsibility.
- Ethical dilemmas: e.g. discovering a vulnerability—report, sell, or stay silent.
- Organizational culture: security culture vs. blame culture; actions to improve culture.
- A “ready for the computer” checklist.

After completing each worksheet, students are ready to start the corresponding computer-based project with a clear idea of the concepts, scenarios, and critical thinking involved.
