# Cyber Strategy – Worksheets (Grade 12)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of cyber strategy fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Cyber strategy from a technical angle: threat modeling, risk assessment, security architecture.
- Threat modeling and risk: threat actors, attack surfaces, risk ranking.
- Defense-in-depth: layered security and why multiple layers matter.
- Incident response: Detect, Respond, Recover; ordering steps in a breach scenario.
- Security trade-offs: cost vs. security vs. usability.

### Social worksheet

- Cyber strategy from a social angle: people, policy, culture, ethics.
- Human factors in security: phishing, awareness; how behaviour affects safety.
- Policy and responsibility: individual, organization, government; shared responsibility.
- Ethical dilemmas: e.g. discovering a vulnerability—report, sell, or stay silent.
- Organizational culture: security culture vs. blame culture; actions to improve culture.
