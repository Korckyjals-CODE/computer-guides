# School Tech – Worksheets (Grade 1, Week 34)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of school technology fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Name common school technology tools (e.g. computer, tablet, screen, headphones, mouse, touch).
- Describe what school tech can do to support learning (show, play sounds, practice skills).
- Explain why asking a teacher is a safe first step when something looks wrong or confusing.
- Connect headphones and quiet settings to not bothering others during work time.
- Practice safe handling: gentle use, not yanking cords, telling a grown-up about loose cables or frozen screens.

### Social worksheet

- Explain why taking turns matters when devices are shared.
- Recognize that classmates’ screens are private unless it is agreed group work; ask before looking or touching.
- Identify kind words and actions that support others who are learning with technology.
- Relate classroom rules (e.g. how to carry devices) to safety and fairness for everyone.
- Reflect on what to do when unsure about a rule or a social situation involving school tech.
