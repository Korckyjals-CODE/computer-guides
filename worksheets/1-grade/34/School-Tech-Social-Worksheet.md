## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** School technology is shared by many people. In this worksheet you will learn how to take **turns**, use **kind words**, respect others’ work on screens, and follow **classroom rules** when devices are part of the day.

## Part 2 – Fair turns (about 5 minutes)

When the class shares tablets or computers, people take **turns**. Waiting can be hard, but turns help everyone get a chance. You can use calm words or quietly read until it is your turn.

**Real-life scenario:** Two friends reach for the same tablet at the same time. The teacher is helping another group.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** What could each friend say or do so the problem feels fair?

**Do this:**

1. **Write** one calm thing you can do while you wait for a turn (e.g. take a deep breath, look at a book).

2. **Draw** a smile in the box to show “fair turns help our class.”

## Part 3 – My work, your screen (about 5 minutes)

What is on a classmate’s **screen** is their work unless the teacher says it is **group work**. **Peek** means looking without permission. It is polite to **ask first** before you touch someone else’s device or look at their answers.

**Real-life scenario:** You finish early and want to see your friend’s game level on their tablet. They did not invite you to look.

**Critical thinking:** Is peeking at their screen without asking usually **okay** or **not okay**? Write one reason for your answer.

_________________________

**Do this:** **Circle** the kinder choice: *Look without asking*  *Say, “Can I look when you are ready?”*

## Part 4 – Kind words while we learn (about 5 minutes)

People learn at different speeds with school tech. **Kind words** encourage others. Laughing at mistakes on a screen can hurt feelings. **Helping** can mean showing where a button is—only if your teacher says peer help is allowed.

**Real-life scenario:** A classmate taps the wrong icon and the whole class sees it. Someone giggles loudly.

**Discuss with a partner or think:** How might that classmate feel? What is one **kind** thing someone could do or say next?

**Do this:** **Write** one encouraging sentence you could say to a friend who is stuck (e.g. “We can ask the teacher together.”).

## Part 5 – Rules keep us safe and fair (about 5 minutes)

Your teacher sets rules for **where** devices go, **when** they are open, and **how** we walk in the room with them. Rules protect people and equipment. If you forget a rule, you can **listen**, **try again**, and **ask** when you are unsure.

**Real-life scenario:** The class is supposed to carry tablets with two hands. You see someone holding theirs with one hand while running.

**Critical thinking:** Why might the **two-hands** rule matter for classmates and the device?
