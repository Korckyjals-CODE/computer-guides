## Part 2 – Kinds of benefits (about 5 minutes)

Technology can help people in different ways. Four common benefit types are:

- **Learn** — find information, practice skills, or follow lessons.
- **Communicate** — share messages, calls, or videos with others.
- **Save time / get things done** — finish tasks faster or with less effort.
- **Stay safe or healthy** — warnings, locks, lights, or tools that protect health.

## Part 3 – Before and after (about 5 minutes)

A benefit often shows up when you compare **before** (without the tool) and **after** (with the tool).

**Real-life scenario:** For a country report, Maria used only library books. She could find good facts, but it took a long time to search many shelves. The next year, her class also used a trusted school search tool. She still had to **choose good sources**, but she found facts faster.

## Part 4 – Right tool for the benefit (about 5 minutes)

Different tools give different benefits. The **best** tool fits the job—not always the newest or flashiest one.

**Real-life scenario:** You want to tell a cousin abroad “Happy birthday” tonight. You could write a letter, make a poster, call on the phone, or use a video call. Each choice has a different benefit (speed, personal touch, seeing faces).

## Part 5 – Benefits need care (about 5 minutes)

Benefits do not appear by magic. Tools need **power** (electricity or batteries), **care** (charging, gentle use, updates), and **wise use** (knowing when to turn them off). A broken or misused tool may give little or no benefit.

**Scenario:** A class tablet will not charge. Students cannot use the homework app until it is fixed. The **benefit** of practice at home is lost until the tool works again.

**Critical thinking:** Name two care habits that keep technology helpful. Why is “more screen time” not always an extra benefit?
