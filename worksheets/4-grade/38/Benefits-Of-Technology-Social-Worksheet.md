## Part 1 – What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

**What you will learn:** Technology’s **benefits** affect **people**—family, friends, classmates, and your community. Using helpful tools in a good way means **sharing fairly**, acting with **kindness**, and keeping **balance** between screens and other parts of life. In this worksheet you will think about who gains from technology, how to share benefits, and how to notice when not everyone has the same access.

## Part 2 – Sharing benefits fairly (about 5 minutes)

When devices or tools are **shared**, everyone should get a fair chance to enjoy the benefit. Keeping extra time or skipping someone’s turn limits what others can learn or do.

**Real-life scenario:** During research time, one student keeps the class tablet for the whole period because “I am not finished yet.” Other students never get to look up their facts.

## Part 3 – Benefits that connect people (about 5 minutes)

Some benefits are **social**: they help people feel close, included, or understood. Examples include video calls with family far away, shared documents for group work, and school messages that keep families informed.

**Scenario:** A grandparent learns to use video calls. Now they can see grandchildren’s faces and hear their voices even when they live in another city.

**Critical thinking:** Why is **looking at the camera** and **speaking clearly** a social skill—not only a “tech” skill?

## Part 4 – When not everyone has the same access (about 5 minutes)

A benefit is greater when **more people** can use it. Some homes do not have the same devices, internet, or quiet space. Schools and communities can offer **more than one way** to take part so fewer people are left out.

**Real-life scenario:** A teacher sends a homework notice **only** through an app. One family does not have a smartphone. They miss the notice until the next day. Another class also sends a **paper copy** home—more families get the benefit of knowing on time.

## Part 5 – Wise and kind use of helpful tech (about 5 minutes)

Helpful technology does not replace good manners. **Ask permission** before using someone else’s device or filming them. Use **kind words** in messages and games. Tell a **trusted adult** if something online feels scary or mean. **Balance** means making time for rest, play outside, reading, and face-to-face talk—not only screens.

**Scenario:** You are using a helpful learning app. A friend starts filming you without asking. You feel uncomfortable.
