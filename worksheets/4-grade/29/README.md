# Protection – Worksheets (Grade 4)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets **whenever** the topic of protection fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work **offline**, with **pen and paper** (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About **25 minutes** per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3–6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it **on their own**; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; TBox projects may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- What protection means (keeping devices and information safe).
- Passwords and lock screens: strong vs weak passwords; why we lock screens when stepping away.
- Secure websites: lock icon (HTTPS), trusted vs unknown sites; critical thinking about fake warnings.
- What to protect: personal information (name, address, school, photos, passwords) and who to share it with.
- Protection habits: updates, backups, not clicking unknown links.

### Social worksheet

- Who to trust: trusted adults vs strangers; friends may accidentally share.
- When friends ask for your password: why sharing weakens protection; saying no kindly.
- Protecting others: telling a grown-up about strange messages, not spreading rumours, helping friends.
- Your choices matter: how to say no; setting a good example for others.
