Part 1 - What you will learn (about 3 minutes)

What you will learn: A system is a group of parts that work together to do a job. In this worksheet, you will learn how systems use input, process, and output, how system parts connect, how to find problems, and how to improve a system.

Do this:

  1. Write one system you use in daily life (e.g. school cafeteria, traffic lights, washing machine).
  2. In one sentence, explain what makes it a system.

Part 2 - Input, process, output (about 5 minutes)

Many systems follow this pattern: input (what goes in), process (what happens to it), and output (the result).

Example: In a toaster, bread goes in (input), it heats up (process), and toast comes out (output).

Real-life scenario: The school office uses a printer system. A student sends homework from a tablet (input). The printer reads the file and prints it (process). A paper copy comes out (output).

Discuss with a partner or reflect in writing: What could cause the output to fail, even if the input is correct?

Part 3 - Parts and connections (about 5 minutes)

In a system, each part has a role. If one part does not work, the whole system may slow down or stop. Good systems have clear connections between parts.

Scenario: A school bell system has a timer, a control box, and speakers. The timer works, but one cable is loose, so one hallway does not hear the bell.

Critical thinking: Why can one small connection change the result for many people?

Part 4 - Finding and fixing problems (about 5 minutes)

Engineers often troubleshoot by checking one part at a time. This helps find the root cause (the main reason for the problem), not just a symptom.

Discussion question: Why is it better to change one thing at a time instead of changing everything at once?

Part 5 - Making systems better (about 5 minutes)

A system can be improved to be faster, safer, or easier to use. Improvement means comparing options and choosing what works best for people.

Real-life scenario: The line at the school lunch system is very slow. Students wait too long and lose recess time.
