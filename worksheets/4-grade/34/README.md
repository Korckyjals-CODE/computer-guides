# Systems - Worksheets (Grade 4)

## For the teacher

- **Use:** Use these worksheets whenever the topic of systems fits your curriculum. They are self-contained learning activities, not tied to a specific computer project.
- **Format:** Students work offline, with pen and paper (and color pencils if you like). No computer or internet needed.
- **Time:** About 25 minutes per worksheet. Each part has a suggested time (roughly 3-6 minutes) so students can pace themselves.
- **Guidance:** The text is written so that students can complete it on their own; no teacher guidance is required during the activity.
- **Standalone value:** Each worksheet teaches on its own. No computer project is required; project work may complement them optionally.

## Learning objectives

### Technical worksheet

- Understand a system as connected parts working together.
- Identify input, process, and output in real-life systems.
- Analyze how parts and connections affect system performance.
- Use a simple troubleshooting sequence to locate root causes.
- Propose and compare improvements to make systems faster, safer, fairer, or easier to use.

### Social worksheet

- Understand social systems as people, roles, and rules working together.
- Explain how individual actions affect fairness and outcomes for others.
- Design practical rules that improve fair access in shared spaces.
- Recognize the role of clear communication in keeping systems effective.
- Propose and evaluate improvements that benefit the whole group.
